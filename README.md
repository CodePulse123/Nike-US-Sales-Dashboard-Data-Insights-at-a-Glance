**Nike US Sales Dashboard – Data Insights at a Glance**
**Overview**

The Nike US Sales Dashboard provides a clear, visual snapshot of Nike's sales performance across the United States. This dashboard is designed to give stakeholders quick access to key metrics, trends, and product insights to drive strategic business decisions.
It combines numerical KPIs, charts, and geographic visualizations to deliver a comprehensive view of sales performance by region, state, product, and category.

**Key Metrics (as per current dashboard)**

1.Total Sales: $8,629,275

2.Total Units Sold: 241,984

3.Average Sales per Transaction: $921.93

4.Average Unit Price: $35.66

**Dashboard Components**
Sales Trend Over Time (Line Chart)
Displays monthly sales trends based on invoice date.
Helps identify seasonal peaks and low periods.
Sales by Region (Bar Chart)
Shows total sales by geographic regions: West, Northeast, Southeast, South, Midwest.
Highlights high-performing regions.
Top Products by Sales (Horizontal Bar Chart)
Highlights the top-selling products: Men’s Apparel, Men’s Athletic Footwear, Men’s Street Footwear, Women’s Apparel, Women’s Street Footwear.
Shows contribution of each product to total sales.
Sales by State (Choropleth Map)
Displays sales distribution across US states using color intensity.
Sales vs Units Relationship (Scatter Plot)
Category Share (Pie Chart)->Shows proportion of sales by product category.

**Data Source**

Dataset: US Nike sales dataset (Excel/CSV)(kaggle dataset)


**Tools Used**

Tableau Desktop – For visualization and dashboard creation

**How to Use**

Open the dashboard in Tableau.

Use filters (if available) to drill down by:

Region

State

Product Category

Time Period

Hover over charts for tooltips showing detailed data.

Interact with charts to cross-filter other visualizations for deeper insights.

**Insights**

The West and Southeast regions show the highest sales.
Men’s Street Footwear is the top revenue-generating product.
California, Texas, and Florida are key states contributing significantly to sales.
Product category “Other” constitutes the largest share, followed by Women’s Apparel.
